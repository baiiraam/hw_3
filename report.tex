\subsection{Categorical encoding}

\textbf{One-Hot Encoding:} Custom \texttt{OneHotEncoder} with \texttt{drop='first'} applied to sex (1 column) and embarked (2 columns).

\textbf{Target Encoding:} CV-safe \texttt{TargetEncoder} with Laplace smoothing demonstrated on embarked using survival as target.

\subsubsection*{Leakage Explanation}
Fitting target encoder on full dataset before splitting causes leakage because test target values influence category means. Our implementation computes per-category means using only training data in \texttt{fit()}, with internal cross-validation in \texttt{fit\_transform()}.

\textbf{RobustScaler} is least affected by heavy-tailed distributions because it uses median and IQR instead of mean and standard deviation. Extreme outliers pull the mean and inflate std, but have minimal impact on median and IQR.

\subsection{Polynomial and interaction features}

\texttt{PolynomialFeatures(degree=2)} applied to MedInc and AveRooms:

\begin{lstlisting}
Original features: (100, 2) → Polynomial features: (100, 6)
Terms: bias, MedInc, AveRooms, MedInc^2, MedInc*AveRooms, AveRooms^2
\end{lstlisting}

For sample point (MedInc=3.0, AveRooms=5.0):
\begin{lstlisting}
bias: 1.00, MedInc: 3.00, AveRooms: 5.00, MedInc^2: 9.00,
MedInc*AveRooms: 15.00, AveRooms^2: 25.00
\end{lstlisting}

\textbf{Interaction term} captures joint effects — house value may depend on income \emph{and} room count together, which additive models cannot capture.

\subsection{Binning (discretisation)}

\texttt{KBinsDiscretizer(n\_bins=10)} with uniform and quantile strategies applied to MedInc:

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.75\textwidth]{binning_comparison.png}
\caption{Uniform vs Quantile Binning}
\label{fig:binning}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Quantile binning} gives more balanced bin sizes by placing equal samples per bin. Uniform binning creates empty bins in skewed distributions. Balanced bins are important when categories are treated as independent features.

\textbf{Data-Ink Ratio:} Applied by removing unnecessary gridlines, redundant labels, chart junk, and using minimal axis decorations.

\textbf{Perceptual-Channel Ordering:} Following Cleveland \& McGill: position along common scale (box plots), length (count bars), area/color (scatter points).

\textbf{What I would change:} Switch from default matplotlib colors to color-blind-safe palettes (e.g., \texttt{sns.set\_palette('colorblind')}), making visualizations accessible to 1 in 12 men with color vision deficiency.

\section{Integration \& Evaluation}

\subsection{Preprocessing pipeline}

Custom sklearn Pipeline built with wrappers for custom transformers:

\begin{lstlisting}[caption=Pipeline Structure]
Pipeline([
    ('preprocessor', ColumnTransformer([
        ('num', Pipeline([
            ('missing_indicator', MissingIndicatorWrapper()),
            ('imputer', CustomSimpleImputerWrapper(strategy='median')),
            ('scaler', StandardScaler())
        ]), ['age', 'pclass', 'sibsp', 'parch', 'fare']),
        ('cat', Pipeline([
            ('imputer', SimpleImputer(strategy='most_frequent')),
            ('onehot', CustomOneHotWrapper(drop='first'))
        ]), ['sex', 'embarked'])
    ])),
    ('classifier', LogisticRegression(max_iter=1000))
])
\end{lstlisting}

\textbf{Cross-Validation Results:}
\begin{lstlisting}
5-Fold CV Accuracy: Fold 1: 0.8252, Fold 2: 0.7902, Fold 3: 0.8028,
Fold 4: 0.7254, Fold 5: 0.8099
Mean Accuracy: 0.7907 (+/- 0.0346)
Test Accuracy: 0.8212
Improvement vs Baseline: +2.40 percentage points
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Leakage checklist}

\textbf{1. Imputation fitted only on training data:} \texttt{SimpleImputer.fit(X\_train)} only, then \texttt{transform(X\_test)}.

\textbf{2. Target encoding with cross-validation:} \texttt{TargetEncoder} uses only training data in \texttt{fit()}. Test categories get training means or global mean.

\textbf{3. Scaling fitted only on training data:} \texttt{StandardScaler.fit(X\_train)}, then \texttt{transform(X\_test)}.

\textbf{Additional:} Missing indicator from training distribution; one-hot categories from training only.

\subsection{Outlier treatment}

Winsorization at 1st and 99th percentiles (fitted on training data):

\begin{table}[H]

Winsorization improves generalization by reducing outlier influence, stabilizing coefficients, and reducing cross-validation variance.
